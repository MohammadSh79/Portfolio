Experiences

During my time as a teaching assistant, I had the privilege of supporting multiple courses in the University of Guilan. These courses included Microprocessors and Assembly Language, Principle of Computer and Programming, Advanced Programming, and Data Structures. As a teaching assistant, I helped students, and graded assignments and exams. This experience not only improved my ability to effectively communicate complex concepts, but also reinforced my passion for imparting knowledge and fostering academic growth.

Parallel to my teaching assistant role, I entered the world of research. One notable project of mine is "Knowledge Distillation for Sperm Anomaly Detection in Limited Data Settings" a research paper that is currently being reviewed for publication. This study explores the application of knowledge distillation techniques to tackle the challenge of detecting sperm anomalies in scenarios where the available data is scarce. By distilling knowledge from a pre-trained model to a smaller student model, we aimed to achieve accurate anomaly detection even with limited data. The research involved analyzing various algorithms, conducting experiments, and evaluating the results. This project not only allowed me to contribute to the scientific community but also deepened my understanding of machine learning and its potential applications in healthcare.
